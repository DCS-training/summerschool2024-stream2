common in the low countries.  The women, among the com-
mon people, come abroad to church and fair, without caps or
bonnets on their heads; and, instead of cloaks or mantles, are
often dressed in striped blankets; such as are used for beds.
The last particularly strikes and offends the eye of a stranger.
An English woman, who came among us lately, said, this dress
always put her in mind of M'Beth's witches at Drury-Lane.
Sirnames are here more numerous, in proportion to the popula-
tion, than in the Highlands, but less numerous than in the
southern districts of Britain.
