     Stipend, Poor, etc.-The value of the living, including the
glebe, is about L. 100 Sterling.  The Crown is patron.
The manse was rebuilt in 1779; the church rebuilt in
1788.  The number of poor who receive alms is between
20 and 30.  The annual Sunday collections, at an average,
amount to about L. 30 Sterling.  Besides this, there is about
L. 200 Sterling lent out, the interest of which is annually
applied to the supply of the poor.

     Prices and Wages.-Beef, veal, mutton, lamb, pork, etc.
sell at about 3 d. or 4 d. the 1 b.; aobut 30 years ago they
sold at about 2 d.  A hen now sells at 7 d. and 8 d.; former-
ly at 4 d. and 5 d.  Eggs are now sold at 3 d. the dozen;
formerly at 1 d.; butter is sold at 7 d. and 8 d. the 1b.; for-
merly at 4 d. and 5 d.  Cheese at 5 s. the stone, formerly at
3 s. or 4 s.  The wages of a day-labourer are 10 d. and 1 s.;
