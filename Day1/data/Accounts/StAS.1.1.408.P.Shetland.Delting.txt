   The language is the same as in the Continent of Scotland.
The inhabitants, however, have less of a provincial brogue
than many parts of North Britain.

   The names of places in the parish, and through all Shet-
land, are derived from the Norwegian language; and those
who understand that language say that the name is, in most
instances, very expressive of the situation and convenience of
the place.
