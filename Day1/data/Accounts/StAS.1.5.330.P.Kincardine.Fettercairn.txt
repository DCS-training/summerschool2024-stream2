     NUMBER XXI.

     PARISH OF FETTERCAIRN,

     (COUNTY OF KINCARDINE.)

     By the Rev. Mr ROBERT FOOTE.

     Name and Situation.

   FETTER signifies a pass, and Cairn a heap of stones,
and there are two large cairns at the top of the mountain,
and many small ones lower down, near to which, according
to tradition, a great battle was fought, from which it is
probable that the district got its name.  The parish is situ-
ated in the presbytery of ForDoun, and Synod of Angus
and Mearns.

   Cultivation, Produce, Soil, Etc.-The ground, on the west
part of the parish, is of a light sharp quality, with some
moss.  On the east, it is of a deeper mould, and some of it
clay.  The usual crops are oats, bear, pease, and occasionally
some wheat.  Turnips are very general, and within the last
15 years, the tenants have made very rapid progress in the
improvement of their farms.  Rents are from 10 s. to L. 1,
7 s. 6 d.  The number of farmers, who pay rent to the pro-
prietors, is 170.  There are several handsome farm houses,
