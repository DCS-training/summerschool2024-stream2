their money this way so much as formerly, they lay out what
they can spare in buying fine cloaths, which is sure a
more venial and excusable way of expending it.  There are
no established inns in either of these islands, although one
might be necessary in the isle of Sanday, for the accommoda-
tion of strangers.  The want of them, however, is not felt
by strangers, as the gentlemen and principal farmers are very
hospitable, and people on business or boatmen, are well lod-
ged at their houses, and pay nothing.
