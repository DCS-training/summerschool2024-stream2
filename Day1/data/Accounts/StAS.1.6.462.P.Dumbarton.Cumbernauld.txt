
     Statistical Account

     NUMBER XLVIII.

     PARISH OF CUMBERNAULD.

     (PRESBYTERY OF GLASGOW, SYNOD OF GLASGOW
     AND AIR, COUNTY OF DUMBARFON.)

     By the Rev. MR. WILLIAM STUART.

     Name, Extent, etc.

   Cumbernauld is of Celtic derivation; cunnur an all
in that language signifying a meeting of streams; and
there is a remarkable collection of springs and streams, slow-
ing in all directions, part running into the Forth and part in-
to the Clyde, within the bounds of this parish, which extends
7 miles in length, and 4 in breadth.  The surface has a ro-
mantic appearance, being beautifully variegated by small hills
and dales.  The highest part of the parish is Funny-side-muir,
a very deep moss, bearing nothing but heath, well stocked
with muir-fowl.  On the south side are two lochs, about a
mile long, and a quarter of a mile broad.  The remainder of
the parish is mostly arable ground, chiefly a heavy clay,though
