Though indulgence over the glass, a characteristic of the
present age, is too frequent, intemperance or rioting is
seldom to be seen on the streets, Religion, it must be re-
gretted, is too much neglected; yet the sabbath-day is de-
cently observed, and divine worship attended by all, except
by those who from ignorances or from habits of irregularity,
are insensible to the solid comforts and satisfaction which
arise from a life of religion and virtue.
