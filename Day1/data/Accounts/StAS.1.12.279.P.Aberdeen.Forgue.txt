
     Of Forgue.

     NUMBER XXII.

     PARISH or FORGUE.

     (COUNTY AND SYNOD OF ABERDEEN, PRESBYTERY OF TUR-
     REFF.)

     By the Rev. Mr. WILLIAM DINGWALL.

     Name, Extent, etc.

   THE ancient name of this parish is Forig, as appears from
an inscription on the communion cups.  The modern
name Forgue.  This name is said to be of Gaelic original, as
are a also many names in this parish and country.  The length
of the parish from N. to S. may be about 9 English miles;
its breadth from W. to E. variable, but the greatest between
5 or 6 miles.  From springs in the hills, descend through the
fields various rivulets, which empty themselves into 2 burns,
namely, the burns of Frendraught and Forgue, the latter of
which is beautifully edged with natural wood.
