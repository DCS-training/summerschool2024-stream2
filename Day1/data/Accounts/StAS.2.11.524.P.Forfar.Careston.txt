  Insects, Worms, Testacea.-Of this numerous,diversified, and
destructive race, we have many kinds, which it is quite unnecessary
to specify. It may, however, be observed that of betles, we possess
a great variety ;-from the Scarabeus melolontha, the cockcbaffer,

down to the Curculio granarius, or weevil, so destructive in grana-
ries. Among these, it may, however, be noticed concerning the Coc-
cinellae,or lady-bird kind, that the juice of these, insects, when
crushed and applied to a carious tooth, is said to give relief, if not to
cure the toothach. It also may be stated, that the Cimex America-
nus, of the American bug, has lately,come, or rather been brought, in
some occult way, to the gardens at the house of Careston, and there
proved most injurios to the fruit trees. Both in gardens, and even
in our fields, we are at times annoyed not a little by different kinds
of slugs, Limaces, more especially by the Limaz agrestis, the small
