Their clothing and furniture are also much better than former-
ly.  In the church of Leslie no person is ever seen in rags.
The young men wear coats of English cloth, fancy vests, etc.
and the young women, printed and white cottons, silk cloaks
and bonnets, etc.  The dress of the maid-servants makes no
inconsiderable addition to the expence of a family in the arti-
cle of washing.  Their furniture also is much better.  About
30 years ago, when the present incumbent was settled, there
was not 6 clocks in the parish, and now there is not a house
in Leslie where there is not either a clock or watch.
