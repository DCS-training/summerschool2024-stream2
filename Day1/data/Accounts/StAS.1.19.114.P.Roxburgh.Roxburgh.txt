     PARISH OF ROXBURGH,

     (COUNTY OF ROXBURGH, SYNOD OF MERSE AND TWEED-
     DALE, PRESBYTERY OF KELSO.)

     By the Rev. Mr Andrew Bell, Minister of the Parish.

   Previous to the Union of the Scotch and English Par-
liaments, this corner of the land was often the feat of
war, and the unhappy scene of broils and feuds, which
usually stigmatize the border inhabitants of two hostile
kingdoms in a barbarous age.  Roxburgh was the residence
and rendezvous of some of the greatest military, political,
clerical and Royal characters Europe has to boast of, and
