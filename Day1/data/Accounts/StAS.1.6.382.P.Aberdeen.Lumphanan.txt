
     Statistical Account

     NUMBER XLI.

     PARISH OF LUMPHANAN.

     (PESBYTERY OF KINCARDINE O'NIEL, SYNOD AND
     COUNTY OF ABERDEEN.)

     By the Rev. Mr. WILLIAM SHAND.

     Name, Extent, and Stipend.

   THE name of the parish in Gaelic signifies it is said,
the "bare little valley;" which might have been the case
when it first received its name; but time has produced great
changes here, in respect of fruitfulness.  Almost in the mid-
dle of the parish stands the church.  The length of the pa-
rish from S. to N. is about 6, in breadth from E. to W. 4
computed miles.-Hills surround the greatest part of the parish.
The soil is deep and good in the low grounds, thin and sandy
on the sides of the hills to the S.-The air is dry and healthy in
general.  Fevers are the most common distempers.-There
are many excellent springs, at the foot of the hill, and in o-
ther parts; so that every village except one, is well supplied
with water.-There is a considerable lake, called the loch of
