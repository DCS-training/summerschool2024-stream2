     By the Rev. Mr. JAMES WILSON.

     Name and General Description.

   In various parts of Scotland, we meet with places
which are named Calder.  The word is probably of
Gaelic derivation, and the meaning of it said to be
"wood and water."  Considering the word in this sense, the
town, from which this parish derives its name, may,
with great propriety, be denominated Calder.  The Al-
mond, a river of considerable size, flows a little to the N.
of it; and 2 small rivers to the E. unite their streams a
few hundred yards before they are mingled with that of
the Almond.  Calder wood, which has one of the above
