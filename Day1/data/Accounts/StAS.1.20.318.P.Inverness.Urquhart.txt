   The inhabitants are attentive to their religious duties,
and, upon the whole, sober, industrous, and virtuous.
   The country contains all the necessaries, and many of the
comforts, of life, in abundance.  It has been considerably
improved already, and improvement is progressive.  The
situation of the people becomes daily better, their living
and clothing are much meliorated, and by perseverence in
the same line of conduct, which at present distinguishes the
superior and inferior, their mutual interest and comfort will
under Providence, be still further promoted and increased.
