     By the Rev. Hugh Meiklejohn, Minister.

     Name.

   Nothing more than probable conjecture can now be
stated with respect to the etymology and signification
of the name of this parish.  The church and village of
Abercorn are situated upon an angular point, and from 60 to
80 feet above the level of the sea.  At the point, about an
hundred yards below the church, the Cornie and Midhope
burns are united, and, after running nearly an hundred
yards farther, between lands of equal elevation with that
upon which the church and village are situated, and which
approach towards each other so as to form a beautiful o-
pening, they fall into the Forth.  Aber, or Aeber, is said
