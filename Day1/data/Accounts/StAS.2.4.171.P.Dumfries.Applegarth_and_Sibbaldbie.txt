        Climate, &c.-It may be remarked, that the temperature must be
considerably influenced by the nakedness of the country, which from
the N. W. round to N. E. is generally destitute of growing wood; a
deficiency, however, which is now in the way of being suppllied. In
the meantime, the cold winds from these quarters recieve no check,
