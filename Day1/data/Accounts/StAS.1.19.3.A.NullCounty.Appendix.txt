   In 1398, Robert Bruce, after being defeated in different
engagements, came to Aberdeen, at which time he was so
exceedingly sick, that he began almost to despair of the re-
covery of his health, as well as of his kingdom; but being
encouraged, and offered assistance, both in men and money,
by the Citizens of Aberdeen, and joined by a considerable
number of them, he went northward in search of the Eng-
lish army, commanded by John Cuming Earl of Buchan,
