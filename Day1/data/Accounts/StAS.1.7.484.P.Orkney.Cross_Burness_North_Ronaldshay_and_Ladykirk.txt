sold from 4 d. to 6 d. each.  Rabbits never sold.  Butter
was then sold from 4 s. to 5 s. per lispund, of 32 pound
Dutch, that is, from 1 1/2 to 2 d. per pound.  Cheese was
never usually made for sale.  Bear or bigg then sold from
2 s. 6 d. to 3 s. per meel, on the bear pundlar; and, as 1 4/6 or
1 5/6 of a meel usually measures a boll, according to the quality
of the grain, bear at 2 s. 6 d per meel, is equal to 4 s. 2 d. or
4 s. 7 d. per boll.  Bear at 3 s. per meel is equal to 5 s. 6 2/3 d.
or 6 s. 1 1/3 d. Sterling per boll.  Oats was never usually sold
until reduced to meal.  Oat-meal then sold from 7 s. 6 d. to
10 s. per meel, on the malt pundlar, which is at present equal
to 11 1/4 stone Dutch; that is, the meel oat-meal at 7 s. 6 d.
per meel, is equal to 4 s. 8 d. per boll, of 8 stone; the meel
oat-meal at 10 s. per meal, is equal to 7 s. 1 1/3 d. per boll.

   Present Prices.-Beef.  A good cow that weighs 2 cwt.
sells for 2 guineas.  If we deduct 10 s. as value of hide and
tallow, there remains 32 s. as the price of 2 cwt. beef, that
is 16 s. per cwt. or from 1 1/2 d. to 1 3/4 d. per pound.  Veal is
not usually fed for sale.-Mutton.  A sheep, that weighs
from 20 to 25 pound, sells from 4 s. to 5 s.  If we deduct
1 s. or 1 s. 6 d. for skin and fleece, this is about 1 1/2 d.
or 1 3/4 d. per pound.  Lambs sell from 1 s. to 2 s. each.-
